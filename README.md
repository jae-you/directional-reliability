### 📌 Abstract

Many machine learning evaluation frameworks define reliable AI primarily in terms of epistemic accuracy, robustness, and harmlessness. As generative AI increasingly mediates human-to-human communication, however, these output-centered notions become insufficient.

This paper introduces **Directional Reliability**, arguing that the reliability of AI mediators depends not only on what the system outputs, but also on **whose communication is being modified and in which direction**. Drawing on an exploratory design probe conducted in the aftermath of South Korea's 2024 martial-law declaration, the paper shows that users often reject AI intervention on their own expression while welcoming optional support for interpreting others' messages.

Rather than treating reliability as a property of model outputs alone, this work proposes a relational perspective that distinguishes sender-side expressive agency from recipient-side communicative scaffolding, with implications for future ML evaluation and AI alignment research.
